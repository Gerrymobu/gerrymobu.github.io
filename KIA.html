KIA emissions project

Currently, laboratory technicians at the emissions department of the KIA Mexico plant have encountered an issue pertaining to the monitoring of pressure levels in comparison gas tanks utilized for emissions testing in their vehicles. These tanks lack a real-time monitoring system for assessing their fill levels or for controlling any potential leaks. Consequently, efforts are underway to devise a solution facilitating tank monitoring through the implementation of a visually intuitive and dependable platform. This solution will integrate sensors and high-quality data acquisition systems to ensure accuracy and reliability in data collection.

To address the equipment issue, ongoing efforts have been directed towards the development of a control device. This device comprises an industrial-level pressure sensor, a Raspberry Pi card, a DAC (Digital-to-Analog Converter), and a visual panel for graphical representation of the measured variables. The aforementioned components are integrated within an Ethernet network, chosen for its efficiency in data transmission and device communication within this application.

Within this project, my role has encompassed the strategic planning and the proposition of components deemed most suitable to ensure optimal data quality and reliability. Additionally, I assumed responsibility for programming and designing the data acquisition process utilizing the Raspberry Pi controller. Furthermore, I spearheaded the creation of a dashboard implemented within the Power BI platform, facilitating intuitive data visualization and analysis.

Personally, this project has been an enlightening journey, revealing the nuances of emissions testing and the necessity of real-time monitoring. It highlights the pivotal role of innovation and meticulous planning in achieving enhanced precision and regulatory compliance within automotive manufacturing. Moving forward, I carry with me invaluable lessons in the pursuit of excellence and industry advancement.
